Biography

Ondre Justus is a rising photographer based in Munich, Germany, whose work is marked by emotional depth and an intuitive connection to his subjects. Originally from Michigan, USA, his journey into photography was anything but conventional—beginning with a rigorous academic background in Biology and Chemistry before a transformative move to Germany reshaped his creative path.

It was in Berlin, surrounded by the city’s raw creativity and unapologetic self-expression, that he rediscovered a long-buried passion for visual storytelling. Photography and film had always been silent loves, but it was in moments of vulnerability—navigating life in a new country, confronting self-doubt, and seeking authenticity—that he realized how profoundly images could articulate what words could not. The camera became both a shield and a mirror, helping him process the world while revealing his own resilience.

His technical precision, honed through scientific training, merges with an artist’s sensitivity, resulting in portraits that feel intimate and alive. Specializing in portraits, small events, and business photography, he thrives on capturing unguarded humanity: the fleeting glance, the quiet confidence, the unscripted joy. Every session is an act of trust, and he approaches it with humility, knowing firsthand how vulnerability can transform into strength.

His journey has been arduous—marked by reinvention, cultural adaptation, and the courage to pursue passion over convention—but it’s these very struggles that infuse his work with authenticity. Now, as a rising talent in Munich’s creative scene, he embraces photography not just as a craft, but as a lifeline: a way to connect, heal, and remind others that even in chaos, there’s beauty worth framing.
