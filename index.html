About me

Ondre Justus is a rising photographer based in Munich, Germany, whose focus is capturing raw and meaningful experiences through the lens of his camera. Originally from Michigan, his path to photography was far from traditional—he started with a strong academic background in Biology and Chemistry before discovering his true passion for visual storytelling.

A huge inspiration that influenced his career change came from several visits to Berlin. He was struck by the city's raw creative energy and openness to self-expression. Photography and film had always been a quiet interest, something he enjoyed but never seriously pursued. However, walking through Berlin's vintage streets, surrounded by art and creativity, really shifted his stance on what was possible.

As time moved forward, navigating cultural shifts and the challenges of building a new life exposed his most vulnerable self. These experiences deepened and reshaped his worldview in profound ways. Living in Germany has given him a unique perspective on change and adaptation. Moving to a new country, learning to navigate different cultural norms, and building a life from scratch taught him that everyone has their own story of growth and reinvention. It was during these most vulnerable moments that he found himself truly pushed toward creative pursuit.

His scientific background became an unexpected asset, teaching him careful observation and attention to detail that now informs his photographic approach. This analytical foundation helps him read lighting, composition, and most importantly, he captures the subtle expressions and body language that unveil his subjects' true inner self.

His approach to photography is straightforward: every session is built on trust and collaboration. He believes the best images emerge from raw, ungaurded moments. In order to do so, he works hard to create a comfortable environment where people can relax and be themselves, understanding from his own experience how important it is to feel safe when being vulnerable. He focuses on capturing people as they truly are—finding those unguarded moments that influence storytelling in a photo.

Now established in Munich, Ondre treats each project as an opportunity to tell someone's story through visual arts. His work reflects his belief that good photography is not only about technical skill, but about creating space for people to be seen and understood exactly as they are.
